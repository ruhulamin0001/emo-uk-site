Declutter and Sell: Turn Your Clutter Into Real Money

21 July 2026 · 5 min read · Checked July 2026

The average UK home has hundreds of pounds of unused stuff sitting in cupboards. Selling it is the fastest money in this whole site, because you already own the products and paid for them years ago.

Here is where to sell what, what each platform takes, and how not to get scared off by the tax noise.

Match the item to the right platform

Clothes, shoes and accessories: Vinted. It is where the buyers are, listing is free, and sellers pay nothing, the buyer covers the small buyer protection fee. This is the single best place for used clothing in the UK.

Electronics, collectables and anything with a model number: eBay. Bigger audience for higher value items, and the auction format finds the true price for rare things. eBay takes a final value fee on most categories, so factor that in.

Furniture and bulky items: Facebook Marketplace. Local, free, and no postage headache because buyers collect. Perfect for anything too big to post.

Books, CDs, DVDs and games: instant sale sites like Ziffit or musicMagpie. They pay less than selling individually, but you scan a barcode, box it up, and they collect for free. The convenience is worth it for low value items you just want gone.

The photos and words that sell

Natural daylight, a plain background, and four or five clear photos beat any clever trick. For clothes, show any flaws honestly, because a returned item costs you more than the small deduction an honest description brings.

Put the brand, size and exact measurements in the listing. Buyers search by those, and a listing without them gets skipped.

List in the evening. That is when most people browse, and fresh listings show near the top.

The tax question, settled

This is where people panic and stop. Selling your own used possessions is not taxable, no matter the total. Clearing your wardrobe, your old phone, unwanted gifts, that is not trading and there is nothing to declare.

You only enter tax territory if you start buying things to resell for profit, or making things to sell. That is a different activity, and the full rules are in our side hustle tax guide.

The scary messages from Vinted and eBay about reporting to HMRC are about the platforms reporting data, not about you owing tax on your old jumpers. Do not let them stop you.

From clear out to actual side hustle

Once your own clutter is gone, some people carry on by sourcing cheap stock to resell. Charity shops, car boot sales and clearance sections can hide items worth far more online.

That is the moment it becomes trading, with tax rules attached, but it is also where a clear out can turn into a genuine small income. The skills are the same: spot value, photograph well, price right.

The money from a good clear out is best not left to drift. Move it somewhere useful the day it arrives, whether that is a card balance or the start of one of the other side hustles on this site, like print on demand.
