VPN in the UK: What You Actually Need and What You Do Not

21 July 2026 · 6 min read · Prices checked July 2026

VPN adverts are everywhere and most of them oversell. Hackers will steal your identity, they say, unless you pay £3 a month right now.

The truth is duller and more useful. A VPN does two or three specific jobs well, and for a lot of people those jobs are worth a few pounds a month. For everyone else it is a subscription doing nothing. Here is how to tell which group you are in.

First, the legal question

Using a VPN is legal in the UK. There is no law against it and there is no sign of one coming. Businesses have used them for decades.

What a VPN does not do is make anything illegal legal. Copyright infringement, fraud and accessing criminal marketplaces are still crimes whatever your IP address says. And getting round a streaming service's regional limits usually breaks their terms of service rather than the law, so the risk there is your account, not the police.

One thing worth knowing: after the Online Safety Act's age verification rules started being enforced, UK VPN signups spiked enormously. Those checks often ask for ID or a face scan on ordinary sites, which is why many people started looking for privacy tools who never cared before.

What a VPN genuinely does

It hides your browsing from your internet provider and from whoever runs the network you are on. On public wifi in a cafe, hotel or airport, that is real protection.

It hides your real location from the sites you visit, which is how people watch content that is not available in their country.

It stops your broadband provider from seeing which sites you visit, which matters more to some people than others.

What it does not do, despite the adverts

It does not make you anonymous. Log into your Google or Facebook account through a VPN and they know exactly who you are.

It does not stop viruses or scams. If you download something bad or enter your card details on a fake site, the VPN watches it happen and does nothing.

It does not protect your banking. UK banking apps and websites are already encrypted. The padlock in your browser was doing that job before you paid anyone.

What it costs in the UK

The honest answer is that prices are strange. A monthly plan is often £10 to £12, while the same service on a two year plan is £1.50 to £3 a month. The long deal is 70% cheaper, which is why almost nobody should buy a single month.

As of July 2026, the well known providers sit roughly here on long plans: Surfshark around £1.50 a month, NordVPN around £2.30, Proton VPN around £2.40. All three are established and have been independently tested. Prices change constantly, so check before buying.

Two warnings on price. First, the cheap headline rate is for the whole term paid upfront, so you will hand over £40 to £70 on day one. Second, renewal is often much more expensive than the introductory deal, so put a reminder in your calendar before it auto renews.

Free VPNs

Mostly avoid them. Running servers costs money, so a free service is being paid another way, usually by logging what you do and selling it. That is the exact opposite of what you came for.

The exception is Proton VPN's free tier, which is genuinely free, run by a company whose paid product is the business model, with no data selling. It is slower and has fewer locations, but for occasional cafe wifi use it is enough.

Do you actually need one

Buy one if you regularly use public wifi, if you travel and want your usual services to work as they do at home, or if you simply do not want your broadband provider logging every site you visit.

Skip it if you only browse at home on your own connection and do not care who knows which sites you visit. That £30 a year is better spent elsewhere, and there are cheaper habits worth changing first, such as the daily coffee that costs £900 a year.

If you run a side hustle you may need one for a real reason: some suppliers, marketplaces and payment tools behave differently by country, and freelancers working with overseas clients often need a stable location. That is a business expense, and business expenses reduce your taxable profit, which is covered in our side hustle tax guide.

How to buy without overpaying

  1. Pick the two year plan, never the monthly one, once you are sure you want it.
  2. Use the money back window, usually 30 days, to test it properly on your own devices.
  3. Set a calendar reminder a week before renewal, because the second year rarely costs what the first did.
  4. Ignore the countdown timers on VPN sites. The deal is always on.
