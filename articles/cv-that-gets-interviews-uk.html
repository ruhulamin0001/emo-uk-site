Write a UK CV That Actually Gets Interviews

21 July 2026 · 5 min read · Checked July 2026

Most CVs are rejected in seconds, and often not by a person. Getting an interview is less about being the best candidate and more about getting past the filters and holding a busy recruiter's attention for the six seconds they give you.

Here is what a UK CV needs in 2026, and the errors that sink good people before anyone reads a word about them.

The filter you have to beat first

Many employers use software to scan CVs before a human sees them. It looks for the keywords from the job advert. If the advert says stock control and customer service and your CV says neither, the software may bin you no matter how suited you are.

The fix is simple and honest: read the advert, and where it is genuinely true of you, use its exact words. Do not lie, mirror. This one habit gets more people through than any clever design.

What a strong UK CV contains

Contact details at the top: name, phone, email, and your town. No need for a full address or a photo, and no date of birth.

A short profile, three or four lines, saying who you are and what you offer, tuned to the specific job. This is the six second hook.

Work history, most recent first, with dates. Under each role, bullet points that show results, not duties. Not responsible for sales but increased sales by 15% over six months. Numbers catch the eye.

Skills, education and anything relevant, kept tight. Two pages maximum, one page if you are early in your career.

Results, not a job description

The biggest difference between a weak CV and a strong one is this. A weak CV lists what the job involved. A strong one shows what you achieved in it.

Anyone in your old role had the same duties. What made you good was the outcome. Handled customer queries becomes resolved over 50 customer queries a day with a 95% satisfaction score. Even rough numbers beat none.

The mistakes that get good people rejected

Sending the same CV everywhere. A generic CV reads as generic. Ten minutes tailoring each one beats fifty copies of the same file.

Typos and inconsistent formatting. They signal carelessness even when you are not careless. Read it aloud, and have someone else check it.

An unprofessional email address. Make a plain firstname.surname address for job hunting.

Unexplained gaps left as a mystery. A short honest line about a gap is better than a suspicious blank.

Burying the recent and relevant under old, irrelevant history. The reader decides in seconds, so put your best and most recent first.

Tools and the honest truth about them

CV builder websites and templates can make a tidy layout quickly, and a clean template does help you pass the scanners. But a beautiful template with weak content still fails. The words matter more than the design.

Free templates in your word processor are perfectly good. You do not need to pay for a fancy design to get an interview.

After the CV

A tailored CV gets you read. A short, specific covering note gets you remembered. Two or three sentences on why this job, at this company, from you.
