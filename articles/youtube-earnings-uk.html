YouTube Earnings UK: What 1,000 Views Is Really Worth

21 July 2026 · 6 min read · Figures checked July 2026

Search how much YouTubers earn and you get numbers from people with millions of subscribers. Useless if you are starting from zero in a bedroom in Bolton.

Here are the actual UK figures, what you have to hit before earning anything at all, and the honest answer to whether it is worth your evenings.

What you have to reach before you earn a penny

To join the YouTube Partner Program and run ads you need 1,000 subscribers plus 4,000 hours of watch time in the last 12 months. That watch time is the hard part. It means roughly 240,000 minutes of people actually watching, not clicking away.

Shorts are counted separately. Their route is 1,000 subscribers plus 10 million Shorts views in 90 days. And this catches people out: Shorts watch time does not count toward the 4,000 hour threshold for the normal route. If you post both, only your long videos build that number.

Most channels take six months to two years to get there, and many never do.

What UK views are actually worth

The number that matters is RPM, which is what you keep per 1,000 views after YouTube takes its 45% cut.

UK creators in 2026 typically see £1.50 to £8 RPM, and where you land depends almost entirely on your subject. Finance, business and tech sit at the top because advertisers pay more to reach those viewers. Gaming, entertainment and comedy sit at the bottom, often near £1.50, because the audience is huge and cheap to reach.

Shorts are a different world. UK Shorts pay pennies per thousand views. Shorts are for growing an audience, not for income.

The maths on a real channel

Say you build a channel to 50,000 views a month, which is a genuine achievement and takes most people a year or more.

At £2 RPM, in gaming or entertainment, that is £100 a month.

At £6 RPM, in finance or business, the same 50,000 views is £300 a month.

Now count the work. Twelve videos a month, each taking four hours from filming to upload, is 48 hours. The gaming channel is earning about £2 an hour. The finance channel is earning about £6 an hour, still below minimum wage.

That is not a reason to quit. It is a reason to understand what ad revenue is: a slow foundation, not the actual business.

Where YouTube money really comes from

Ask any UK creator earning a living and ads will be a minority of their income.

Sponsorships pay far better. A channel with 10,000 engaged subscribers in a specific niche can charge £200 to £800 for an integrated mention, and that is one video, not a month of them.

Affiliate links work quietly in the background. A tech or money channel linking the products it genuinely uses can out earn its own ad revenue, which is the same logic behind our VPN guide.

Your own product beats both. Courses, templates, printables. The audience is already there and you keep almost everything.

This is the pattern across every side hustle on this site. Platform payouts are the entry level, and the real money is in what you build on top of the audience.

Choosing a subject with the money in mind

Pick something where the viewer is close to spending money. Personal finance, software, business tools, home improvement, and anything involving expensive kit all attract higher paying adverts.

Also pick something you can still film in month eighteen. Almost every channel that fails does so because the person got bored, not because the algorithm buried them.

The overlap between those two, a subject with commercial value that you would happily discuss for free, is where a channel actually works.

The tax side

YouTube pays you as a self employed individual. Once your total income from it and any other side work passes £1,000 in a tax year, you must register for Self Assessment. Details are in our side hustle tax guide.

Keep receipts for the camera, microphone, lighting and editing software. Those are legitimate business expenses that reduce the profit you pay tax on.

Should you start

Start if you want to build an audience and can accept earning nothing for the first year. That is the honest entry fee.

Do not start if you want money within three months. For that, our remote jobs guide pays this month rather than next year.

But if you do stick with it, the audience becomes an asset that nobody can take away, and the ad money turns out to be the least valuable thing it gives you.
