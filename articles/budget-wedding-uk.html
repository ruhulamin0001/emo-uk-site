Budget Wedding UK: A Real Day for a Fraction of £20,000

21 July 2026 · 6 min read · Costs checked July 2026

The average UK wedding now costs £20,604, according to the 2026 industry figures. That number frightens people into debt before they have even set a date.

Here is the reassuring truth. A quarter of UK couples spend £10,000 or less, and a genuinely lovely day can be done for far less than that. The trick is knowing which three decisions control almost the entire bill.

The legal minimum, so you know the floor

A legal marriage at a register office costs about £56 in 2026: the ceremony fee plus giving notice. That is the true minimum to be legally married in the UK.

Nobody is suggesting you do only that, but it helps to know the floor. Everything above £56 is a choice, not a requirement, and seeing it that way changes how you spend.

The three decisions that set the whole bill

Ignore the hundred small line items for a moment. Three choices decide roughly 80% of what you pay.

Guest numbers. This is the biggest lever by far. Every guest means more food, more drink, a bigger venue and more of everything. Cutting from 100 guests to 40 does not cut the cost by a bit, it transforms it. A small wedding is not a compromise, it is the single most powerful saving there is.

The day and the season. A Saturday in summer averages £22,290. A weekday in winter can be thousands less for the exact same venue. Venues discount hard for Tuesdays, Wednesdays and off season dates because they struggle to fill them. Wednesday and Thursday weddings are growing fast among couples who did the maths.

The venue type. A dedicated wedding venue bundles in a premium. A village hall, a pub function room, a botanical garden, a family garden or a restaurant's private room can cost a fraction and often looks better in photos precisely because it is not a wedding factory.

A realistic £4,000 to £6,000 day

Here is how 40 guests works in practice.

  1. Register office or village hall ceremony and hire: £300 to £600
  2. Catering, a hot buffet or a shared feast rather than a plated three course meal: £1,200 to £2,000
  3. Drinks, welcome drink plus wine on tables, no full open bar: £400 to £800
  4. Photography, a talented newer photographer rather than a big name: £600 to £1,200
  5. Dress, sample sale or preloved: £150 to £600
  6. Flowers, seasonal and simple or silk: £150 to £400
  7. Cake, music, stationery and extras: £400 to £800

That lands between roughly £3,600 and £6,400 for a full, warm, proper wedding day. The same event billed as a package at a Saturday summer venue would clear £15,000.

Where the real savings hide

Do not have a full open bar. It is the quiet budget killer. A welcome drink and wine on the tables is plenty, and guests rarely notice.

Buy the dress preloved or from a sample sale. Gowns worn once sell for a fraction of retail, and rental is perfect if you will never wear it again.

Skip the wedding label. The moment a florist, caterer or car hire firm hears the word wedding, the quote rises. Ask for a party or a private event where you honestly can.

Let people help. A friend who bakes, an uncle with a nice car, a cousin who is handy with a camera for the casual shots. Involving people is not cheap, it is personal, and it is what guests remember.

Where not to cut

Photography is the one thing that lasts after the day ends. Go cheaper than a celebrity photographer, but do not go for the cheapest, because bad photos are the one regret that cannot be fixed.

And do not go into high interest debt for a single day. A wedding paid for on a credit card at 25% interest can take years to clear. If you are borrowing, understand what it truly costs, which we cover in our guide to UK cashback credit cards.

Funding it without a loan

Give yourself a year and the maths gets gentle. £5,000 over twelve months is about £420 a month between two people, which is £210 each. That is roughly what two people spend on bought coffee and lunches, as our coffee cost breakdown shows.

Set up a separate savings pot, pay in on payday before anything else, and the day funds itself without a single penny of debt.
